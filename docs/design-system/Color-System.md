# Color System

## Purpose

The Music Factory color system protects the brand from looking like a SaaS product, a startup dashboard, or a technology demo. Color must support Editorial, Museum, Luxury, Archive, Music Civilization, Humanity, Quiet, and Contemporary expression.

## Core Principles

- Warmth before brightness.
- Depth before saturation.
- Restraint before decoration.
- Editorial contrast before interface contrast.
- Cultural atmosphere before product utility.

## Primary Palette

| Token | Value | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |

| `color.paper` | `#fffdf8` | Elevated paper surface. |

## Usage Rules

- Use warm white and paper as the dominant environment.
- Use charcoal for authority and legibility.
- Use warm gray for metadata and secondary rhythm.
- Use dark gold only for controlled emphasis, section labels, timeline markers, or symbolic accents.
- Avoid pure white fields that feel sterile.
- Avoid cold gray systems that feel like dashboards.
- Avoid bright blue, neon cyan, purple-blue gradients, and glowing outlines.

## Contrast Rules

- Primary text must remain readable on warm white and paper.
- Low contrast may be used only for secondary atmospheric details.
- Metadata may be quieter than body copy but must not become unreadable.
- Hover color should usually shift opacity or underline, not change to a loud color.

## Realm Palette Rules

- Realm palettes must extend the core palette, not replace it with unrelated themes.
- Realm colors must remain compatible with warm white and charcoal.
- Realm colors may influence imagery, accents, and subtle backgrounds.
- Realm colors must not create game UI, fantasy UI, or cyber UI unless explicitly approved.

## Forbidden

- Technology blue glow.
- Startup gradient hero colors.
- Dashboard gray-blue interfaces.
- Glassmorphism color stacks.
- Cyberpunk neon palettes.
- Color used only to make an empty area look busy.

## Review Checklist

- Does the palette feel editorial and cultural?
- Does the screen still read as Music Factory?
- Does the color support content rather than compete with it?
- Is the accent used with restraint?
- Is there any SaaS, dashboard, or startup signal?
